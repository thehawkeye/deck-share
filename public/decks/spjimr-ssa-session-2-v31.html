Bait. Hook. Second hook. Third hook. You only fall for it once.
(Deliberate exception — no textbook name) Forget CLV. The mechanic is bait-and-hook. The first phone is the bait. The exchange is the hook. Accessories, software subscription, the second phone — third, fourth, fifth hook. Compounding lock-in. The channel knows which hook to deploy when. That's why the price mechanic isn't a pricing question — it's a channel question.

Business model — every purchase deepened the platform. Product — every device was a touchpoint. Supply chain — Apple's vertical IP. Brand — none of it was advertising, all of it was word of mouth. Channel — bought half online, half in store, didn't matter. Service — the devices all sync. Ecosystem — that's the word for it.

Theory anchor #4 · Platform Revolution (Parker / Van Alstyne / Choudary) The flywheel effect — every participant on the platform increases its value for every other participant. Apple has ten. Realme has one. Same phone. Different platform. Different gravity.
