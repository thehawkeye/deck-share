The Central Thesis

The moat
has shifted
from
breadth
to depth.

For two decades, platform power came from capturing breadth — more users, more supply, more transactions.

In the agent economy, the competitive moat is depth — how deeply a system understands a user's intent, history, and context, creating unavoidable dependency.

The question is no longer "how many eyeballs?" It is "how deeply are we embedded in outcomes that matter?"

The Architectural Divide

Bounded
or open?
The distinction
determines
survival.

An open agent will eventually bypass aggregators entirely — searching open endpoints to connect a user directly with a provider, completely eroding the platform's margin.

Bounded agents replace your frontend. Open agents replace your entire platform.

Two Architectures
Bounded Agentic

The incumbent's compromise

Agent operates within a single ecosystem's service graph. Platform retains orchestration control. Supply is limited to what the platform owns.

Open Agentic

The existential threat to incumbents

Agent operates across ecosystem endpoints. Discovers supply wherever it exists. Completes transactions without returning to any platform frontend.

For the restaurant owner wanting an alternative to Zomato: she needs open agentic endpoints. If Zomato controls the agent's routing, she pays Zomato's margin regardless of the agent intermediary.

04 / 10
What Agents Cannot Replicate

Agents
cannot
solve
physics.

Incumbents with heavy, real-world fulfilment capabilities hold a massive, agent-resistant advantage.

Agents can optimize routing, reduce friction, and match preferences. But they cannot move a package from point A to point B faster than physics allows.

The agent era does not make fulfilment moats irrelevant. It makes them more valuable, because they are the only moats agents cannot replicate.

Agent-Resistant Evidence
Fulfilment Infrastructure

Amazon Fulfilment Network

Agents can route to Amazon but cannot replicate 2-day fulfilment physics. Amazon becomes the preferred endpoint agents route to.

Physical Services

Urban Company

Platform-profiled, warranty-backed service delivery. Trust + governance + quality enforcement creates a stack agents cannot arbitrage away.

The Asymmetry

Aggregators get disrupted. Fulfilment incumbents get enhanced.

The businesses that survive agentic commerce are the ones whose supply agents need to reach anyway — because physics.

The Trust Imperative

Trust is no
longer a
marketing
promise.

If your organisation cannot express its reliability in auditable, data-driven ways, algorithms will structurally filter you out of agentic routing before a human ever knows you exist.

Unreliable execution becomes a structural disqualifier, not a competitive weakness.

Only trust that can be verified scales. The question is not whether to build trust infrastructure — it is whether your trust infrastructure can be read by machines.

The Verification Layer
ADO — Agent Discoverability Optimization

The new SEO

If you cannot be discovered by agents, you do not exist. Ranking factors: discoverability, reliability, capability, performance.

The Standards Race

Protocol wars have begun

China moved first with Alipay's Agentic Commerce Trust Protocol. The standards gatekeeper tier is now contested territory.

The Practical Implication

Verification is an economic layer, not a brand claim.

Credibility custody moves toward independent verifiers. Verification becomes an explicit transaction layer with cost and certification tiers.

The Delegation Spectrum

Delegation
is already
happening
at scale.

The era of delegated, zero-click purchasing is not a future scenario. It is already operating at scale in production commerce systems.

The question for every business is not whether delegation will happen. It is at what delegation level your business model operates — and who controls that level.

Four Levels of Delegation
Low
Human in loop
Conversation with AI. User confirms each order. The current default.
Moderate
AI recommends
AI recommends. Human approves. Still bounded within ecosystem trust rails.
High
AI executes
User pre-sets conditions — order times, spending limits, preferred merchants. AI acts automatically.
Full
Zero human
Agent renews subscriptions, buys at optimal price, switches providers. No human involved.

An agent managing a household budget notices a price increase, discovers three alternatives, negotiates volume pricing, and switches. All before the owner's morning workout. No procurement team.

Fiduciary vs Influence Economy

Three
questions.
They
determine
everything.

These three determine whether you operate in a fiduciary agent economy or an influence agent economy — and the answer shapes every strategic decision.

The Three Orchestration Control Questions
1
What is the objective function — and whose definition dominates?
2
What are the governance guardrails — what should the agent never do, and how is that enforced?
3
How do learning loops compound — and who owns that accumulation?

Fiduciary: Agent serves user's interest exclusively. Influence: Agent optimises for platform or sponsor. The business model determines which you are.

Subscription Agent

User pays. Agent serves user. Fiduciary model. Highest trust ceiling.

Sponsored Agent

Platform funds agent. Agent optimises for sponsor. Influence model. Disclosure required.

The Migration Imperative

Move
before
routing
commoditises
you.

The pivot window is open but finite. Casualty firms still have a migration path — but only if they build depth before routing gets fully commoditised.

The strategic test is migration speed toward Pivot or Fortress before the window closes.

From platform heuristics + UI-led ranking → to objective function + governance rules + closed-loop learning. From attention architecture → to alignment architecture.

Firm Redesign Principles
1 — Objective Function Sovereignty

Keep it in-house

Who decides what the system optimises for. If this is outsourced, the firm becomes a replaceable supplier in someone else's decision stack.

2 — Governance Stack

Keep it in-house

Guardrails, veto rights, escalation rules, liability boundaries. The firm that defines acceptable outcomes controls the architecture.

3 — Learning Loops

Keep it in-house

Feedback, adaptation cadence, reconfiguration rights. Data portability does not equal intelligence portability. Calibrated inference depth stays sticky.

Externalise commodity execution. Keep decision infrastructure sovereign. Plug into independent verification rails.
