Talk Point 2
The Architectural Divide

Bounded
or open?
The distinction
determines
survival.

An open agent will eventually bypass aggregators like Zomato entirely by searching open internet endpoints to connect a user directly with a restaurant, completely eroding the platform's margin.

Bounded agents replace your frontend. Open agents replace your entire platform.

Two Architectures
Bounded Agentic

Qwen App model

Agent operates within a single ecosystem's service graph. Platform retains orchestration control. Supply is limited to what the platform owns.

Navigates between Taobao, Alipay, Fliggy, Amap — but cannot cross into competing ecosystems.

Open Agentic

The existential threat

Agent operates across ecosystem endpoints, discovers supply wherever it exists, completes transactions without returning to any platform's frontend.

Orchestration control shifts to the agent. The platform becomes invisible.

For the restaurant owner wanting an alternative: she needs open agentic endpoints. If Zomato controls the agent's routing, she pays Zomato's margin regardless of the agent intermediary.

Talk Point 4
The Physics Moat

Agents
cannot
solve
physics.

Incumbents with heavy, real-world fulfilment capabilities hold a massive, agent-resistant advantage.

Agents can optimize routing, reduce friction, and match preferences. But they cannot move a package from point A to point B faster than physics allows.

The agent era does not make fulfilment moats irrelevant. It makes them more valuable, because they are the only moats agents cannot replicate.

Agent-Resistant Evidence
Fulfilment

Amazon Fulfilment Network

1.9 million packages per day through FBA. Agents can route to Amazon but cannot replicate the 2-day physics.

Physical Services

Urban Company

Platform-profiled, pro-rated, warranty-backed service delivery. Agents route to UC; UC owns the physical execution.

The Asymmetry

Bounded disruption vs open survival

Aggregators get disrupted. Fulfilment incumbents get enhanced — their supply becomes the preferred endpoint agents route to.

Talk Point 5
The Central Thesis

The moat
has shifted
from
breadth
to depth.

For two decades, platform power came from capturing breadth — more users, more supply, more transactions.

In the agent economy, the competitive moat is depth — how deeply a system understands a user's intent, history, and context, creating an unavoidable dependency that resists commoditisation.

The question is no longer "how many eyeballs do we capture?" It is "how deeply are we embedded in outcomes that matter?"

Three Futures — Business Classification
Casualty — Eroding

Aggregation + Discovery

Pure plays. Agent replaces frontend without changing supply.

Meesho, ONDC participants

Deep domain depth or physical fulfilment moat

Agents route to you. You become the preferred endpoint. Fulfilment + trust creates unavoidable dependency.

Urban Company, Amazon Fulfilment, Credit card networks

Talk Point 6
The Delegation Shift

This is not
a pilot.
It is
happening.

The era of delegated, zero-click purchasing is already operating at staggering scale.

Alipay's Agentic Commerce Trust Protocol recently processed 120 million AI-mediated transactions in a single week. This is not a beta test. This is production commerce.

The question for every business is not whether delegation will happen. It is at what delegation level your business model operates.

The Delegation Spectrum
Low
Instant Payment
Conversation with AI. User confirms each order. Bounded within ecosystem. Qwen App model.
Moderate
Instant + Review
AI recommends. Human approves. Still bounded within ecosystem trust rails.
High
Delegated Auth
User pre-sets conditions — order times, spending limits, preferred merchants. AI executes automatically.
Full
Zero Human
Agent renews subscriptions, buys at optimal price, switches providers. No human involved.

The practical result: an agent managing a household budget notices a 15% tariff-driven price increase, discovers three local alternatives, requests samples, negotiates volume pricing, and switches. All before the owner's morning workout. No procurement team.

Talk Point 7
Fiduciary vs Influence Economy

Three
questions.
They
determine
everything.

These three determine whether you operate in a fiduciary agent economy or an influence agent economy — and the answer shapes every strategic decision you make.

The Three Orchestration Control Questions
1
What is the objective function — and whose definition dominates?
2
What are the governance guardrails — what should the agent never do, and how is that enforced?
3
How do learning loops compound — and who owns that accumulation?

Fiduciary: Agent serves user's interest exclusively. Influence: Agent optimises for platform or sponsor. The business model determines which you are.

Subscription Agent

User pays. Agent serves user. Fiduciary model. Highest trust requirement.

Sponsored Agent

Platform funds agent. Agent optimises for sponsor. Influence model. Disclosure required.
